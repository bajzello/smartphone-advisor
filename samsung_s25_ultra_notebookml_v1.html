Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra

Released: February 2025

Starting Price: $1299 / 1469 € (Pricing varies by model and region) [1-5]

Ultra-Premium Segment (>$800) [1]

Overview

The Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra is a high-end smartphone that debuted in January 2025 and was released on February 3, 2025 [6, 7]. It is the flagship model of the Samsung brand [7]. It features a durable build with titanium frame and Corning's glare-free glass (Gorilla Glass Armor 2 for the front, Gorilla Glass Victus 2 for the back) [8-11]. Key features include an integrated S Pen, IP68 dust and water resistance, and 45W wired charging [8-10, 12].

While some reviews suggest it plays it safe and might not be the absolute "best" in every single category compared to competitors [13-15], it is described as a very good, or even "awesome" phone [14, 15]. One review calls it the most enticing phone Samsung has ever made and the closest to being an iPhone killer [16]. However, it's also noted that the overall image quality might be below competitors in its price segment [17].

Design and Materials

The design is described as elegant and modern [11], with a durable titanium build [9, 18, 19].

  • Dimensions: 162.8 x 77.6 x 8.2 mm (6.41 x 3.06 x 0.32 inches) [8, 10, 19]. Thickness with camera is 9.1 mm [10].
  • Weight: 218g (7.69 oz) [6, 8, 10]. One review mentions it feels much lighter [20], while another says it looks slightly thicker but is actually thinner [21]. Kimovil lists the weight as 219g [19, 22].
  • Materials: Glass front (Corning Gorilla Armor 2), glass back (Gorilla Glass Victus 2), titanium frame (grade 5) [8, 10]. Titanium build makes it extremely durable [18].
  • Durability: IP68 dust and water resistant (immersible up to 1.5m for 30 min) [8, 10, 19]. Some reviewers note its toughness [23].
  • Colors: Titanium Silver Blue, Titanium Black, Titanium White Silver, Titanium Gray, Titanium Jade Green, Titanium Jet Black, Titanium Pink Gold [8, 10, 19]. The Jade Green is noted as an online exclusive and a favourite color [24].
  • Physical Features: Stylus support (integrated S Pen) [8-10, 25]. Volume and Lock/Unlock keys on the right side [10, 26]. SIM card tray location has been flipped [27].
  • User Interface: Usable screen surface of 91% allows maximum use of screen space [18, 19].

Display

The display is a key highlight, described as one of the best displays and a favourite part of the phone for some reviewers [14, 24].

  • Type: Dynamic LTPO AMOLED 2X [8, 10, 11, 25].
  • Size: 6.9 inches [6, 9, 10]. Kimovil lists 6.86 inches [11, 22, 25]. The screen is slightly larger than the S24 Ultra due to thinner bezels [28, 29].
  • Resolution: 1440 x 3120 pixels (WQHD+) [6, 8, 10, 11, 22, 25]. Described as a 1440p class screen [30].
  • Refresh Rate: 120Hz [8, 11, 25]. Variable refresh rate (VRR) from 1-120Hz [25, 29, 30]. Makes animations and scrolling smooth [11, 30].
  • Brightness: High peak luminance up to 2600 nits [11, 17, 25]. DXOMARK notes excellent readability outdoors thanks to high peak luminance and low reflection [17]. Measured max brightness is 1417 nits [8]. One review notes that in SDR, without auto brightness, it only goes up to ~450 nits, or ~850 nits with an extra brightness toggle [31, 32].
  • Other features: HDR10+ support provides rich colors and deep blacks [8, 11, 25]. Always-on display [8, 25]. DX anti-reflective coating / glare-free glass is highly praised [8, 9, 14, 33-35]. Corning Gorilla Glass Armor 2 protection [8, 10, 11, 25, 29]. Supports S-Pen [25].

Performance and Hardware

Equipped with the latest processor for fast and smooth performance [18].

  • Chipset: Qualcomm SM8750-AB Snapdragon 8 Elite (3 nm) [6, 8, 9, 18, 36, 37]. Described as Snapdragon 8 Elite for Galaxy [9]. This chip is expected to be in many other flagships released in 2025 [38].
  • CPU: Octa-core (2x4.47 GHz Oryon V2 Phoenix L + 6x3.53 GHz Oryon V2 Phoenix M) [8, 36]. Kimovil lists 2x4.32 GHz Oryon V2 Phoenix L + 6x3.53 GHz Oryon V2 Phoenix M [36].
  • GPU: Adreno 830 [8, 39].
  • RAM: 12GB or 16GB (LPDDR5X) [6, 8, 9, 18, 39]. All S25 Ultra models start at 12GB RAM [40].
  • Storage: 256GB, 512GB, or 1TB (UFS Storage 4.0) [6, 8, 9, 18, 39, 41]. No card slot for external memory [6, 39].
  • Benchmark: AnTuTu (v10) score of 3050000 [18]. GSMArena lists AnTuTu (v10) 2207809 [8]. Performance is better than 98% of devices according to Kimovil's AnTuTu score [39]. GeekBench: 9846 (v6) [8]. 3DMark: 6687 (Wild Life Extreme) [8].
  • Cooling: Equipped with a cooling system [42]. Features a 40% larger vapor chamber and a new Thermal Interface Material (TIM) connecting the chip to the vapor chamber [38, 43, 44]. Reviewers found no warming up during regular use [38].
  • Gaming Performance: Performs like a hot damn even at steady state, crushing the previous generation and Pixel 9 Pro [31].

Camera

Features an advanced quad-camera system on the rear and a single selfie camera [42].

Rear Cameras:

  • Main (Wide): 200 MP, f/1.7, 24mm, 1/1.3", 0.6µm, multi-directional PDAF, OIS. Sensor: Samsung ISOCELL HP2 [8, 42, 45].
  • Ultrawide: 50 MP, f/1.9, 120˚, 0.7µm, dual pixel PDAF, Super Steady video. Sensor: Samsung JN3 [8, 42, 45]. This is a genuinely nice upgrade, resulting in sharper images corner to corner and better low light performance [46, 47].
  • Telephoto (3x Optical Zoom): 10 MP, f/2.4, 67mm, 1/3.52", 1.12µm, PDAF, OIS, 3x optical zoom. Sensor: Sony IMX754 [8, 42, 45, 48, 49].
  • Periscope Telephoto (5x Optical Zoom): 50 MP, f/3.4, 111mm, 1/2.52", 0.7µm, PDAF, OIS, 5x optical zoom. Sensor: Sony IMX854 [8, 42, 45, 48, 49].

Both telephoto lenses have OIS [48, 50]. The camera rings around the lenses are slightly more pronounced [28, 51].

Selfie Camera:

  • Single: 12 MP, f/2.2, 26mm, 1/3.2", 1.12µm, dual pixel PDAF. Sensor: Samsung S5K3LU [8, 42, 52].

Camera Features and Video:

  • Optical Zoom: 3x and 5x [48, 50].
  • Optical Quality Zoom: 2x and 10x (Enabled by Adaptive Pixel sensor) [48, 50].
  • Digital Zoom: Up to 100x [48, 50, 53]. Digital zoom at higher levels is mostly a gimmick [53].
  • Stabilisation: OIS on standard, ultrawide, and both telephoto lenses [44, 48, 50, 52].
  • Video Recording: 8K@24/30fps, 4K@30/60/120fps, 1080p@30/60/120/240fps [8, 50, 52]. Features 10-bit HDR, HDR10+, stereo sound rec., gyro-EIS [8]. Log video shooting is supported and praised by reviewers, allowing for more flexible editing [47, 54-57].
  • Other Features: Laser AF, Best Face, LED flash, auto-HDR, panorama, Phase detection autofocus (PDAF), HDR, face recognition, ISO settings, manual white balance, etc. [8, 50, 52]. Night Mode 2.0 [52]. Object eraser feature is noted as impressive [58].

Reviews on camera performance are mixed. DXOMARK notes solid performance in tele and video in bright light conditions but overall image quality below competitors [17]. Some reviews praise daytime photos and specific zoom levels [49, 59] and mention great autofocus [15], while others note poor night photos unless using pro mode [60] and sometimes overprocessed images [15]. The camera UI has improvements making it easier to swap between lenses and settings [53].

DXOMark Score: Mobile 146, Photo 144, Video 143 [48]. User opinions give the camera a 9.1/10 overall [60].

Battery

Features a large battery with fast charging capabilities.

  • Capacity: 5000 mAh (Li-Ion) [6, 8, 9, 12, 22, 61].
  • Wired Charging: 45W wired, PD3.0 [8, 9, 12, 61]. Can reach 65% in 30 min [8]. Bypass charging is supported [12].
  • Wireless Charging: 15W wireless (Qi2 Ready) [8, 12]. Supports wireless charging [12, 61]. Qi2 allows magnetic alignment, but the phone doesn't have built-in magnets; a case is needed [62].
  • Reverse Wireless Charging: 4.5W [8, 12]. Allows charging other devices [12, 61].

Battery life has been reported as awesome [15], even better than last year [63]. Some users end the day with 30-40% left [15]. However, user opinions on battery life are mixed, with some finding it not lasting as long as desired (around 17 hours) [60], and user reviews giving the battery an 8.0/10 overall [60] and a 7.5/10 quality-price score [64]. One review measured about an hour less battery life in video playback compared to the S24 Ultra [30].

Connectivity and Other Features

  • SIM: Nano-SIM + Nano-SIM + eSIM + eSIM (max 2 at a time) for international models, with regional variations [8, 41, 65]. Supports Dual SIM Dual Standby (Nano SIM / eSIM + Nano SIM / eSIM) [65].
  • Network: Supports GSM, CDMA, HSPA, EVDO, LTE, 5G [8]. Wide range of 5G, 4G, 3G bands supported, with international and USA unlocked variants listed [7, 8, 10, 41, 66].
  • Wi-Fi: Wi-Fi 802.11 a/b/g/n/ac/6e/7, tri-band, Wi-Fi Direct [8, 65, 67]. Supports Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax), Wi-Fi 6E, Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be) [65, 67].
  • Bluetooth: 5.4, A2DP, LE [8, 61, 67, 68]. Supports profiles like aptX, aptX-HD, LDAC, SBC, ANT+ [67, 68].
  • Positioning: GPS, GLONASS, BDS, GALILEO, QZSS [8, 67]. Supports GPS (L1+L5), GLONASS (L1), BeiDou (B1), Galileo (E1+E5a), QZSS (L1 + L5) [61, 68]. User opinions rate GPS quality highly (9.6/10) [69].
  • USB: USB Type-C 3.2, DisplayPort 1.2, OTG [8, 67, 69]. Supports Charging, OTG, and USB Host 3.2 [67, 69].
  • Other: NFC, Ultra Wideband (UWB) support [8, 61, 67, 69]. Supports Samsung DeX and Samsung Wireless DeX (desktop experience support) [8]. No 3.5mm headphone jack [8, 67, 69]. Stereo speakers tuned by AKG [8, 18, 45, 67]. Noise cancellation microphones [45, 67].

Software and AI Features

Runs on Android 15 with One UI 7 [6-9, 70, 71]. Samsung promises up to 7 major Android upgrades and 7 years of security patches [6, 8, 70]. One UI 7 is described as potentially the best Android OS [72].

A major focus is on AI features, described as the biggest new features [73]. However, the AI is currently only free until the end of 2025, with unknown costs thereafter [74].

Key AI Features Mentioned:

  • Gemini Integration: Hold to activate Gemini is a step up from Google Assistant [75]. It can handle complex commands [76]. Gemini Extensions allow accessing data from apps like email to add events to the calendar [77].
  • AI Select: Described as a game changer for quickly selecting text from paragraphs or screen grabbing items in chats [75]. Can be mapped to the side button [75].
  • Circle to Search: Still a preferred feature for some users [78].
  • AI Wallpaper Generation: Considered probably the best on any phone, offering fast, high-quality options that match the UI colour palette [79].
  • Multi-step Gemini/Samsung Integration: Allows combining finding information and then performing an action (e.g., finding a restaurant and texting it) [80-82].
  • Daily Briefs (AI): Shows calendar, weather, news, Spotify playlists [73, 77]. Some reviewers found it not very useful or timely [73, 77, 83, 84].
  • Gemini Live: Allows getting context on what's on your screen, including summarising YouTube videos or asking clarifying questions [79, 85, 86].
  • Interpreter Mode: Can facilitate almost real-time conversation in another language, though execution can be flawed [87, 88].
  • Call Recording with AI Summarization: Allows recording calls, searching transcripts, and getting summaries, but the transcription accuracy can be poor [89].
  • Now Bar (AI): Sits on the lock screen, similar to Apple's Dynamic Island [84, 90]. Can provide live updates for specific sports [84, 90, 91]. Some reviewers like its integration with sports and find it convenient [84, 91].
  • Article Summary (AI): Works in Samsung's internet browser and is noted as fast [91].

Reviews on AI features are mixed. Some features are praised (AI Select, AI Wallpaper, multi-step commands, Circle to Search) [75, 78-82], while others are seen as needing improvement or not very useful (Daily Briefs, Interpreter mode, Call summary accuracy) [73, 77, 83, 84, 87-89]. The level of intelligence to understand requests exists, but the context understanding is sometimes missing [90, 92, 93].

S Pen

The S Pen is an integrated, super precise stylus [9, 94]. It has been part of the Ultra line since the S22 [94].

A significant change this year is the removal of the Bluetooth functionality from the S Pen [26, 95, 96]. This means it can no longer be used as a remote camera shutter or for media playback controls [95, 96]. The old S Pen with Bluetooth does not fit in the new phone [95]. Samsung justified this by saying data showed less than 1% of users activated this feature [97]. Some reviewers see this removal as a "sneaky" downgrade [96], going against the "Ultra" concept of adding extra features for enthusiasts [54, 97, 98]. The S25 Ultra appears capable of providing power to the S Pen, but the new S Pen without a battery cannot receive it [99]. It's suggested Samsung might upsell a Bluetooth-enabled S Pen in the future [99].
